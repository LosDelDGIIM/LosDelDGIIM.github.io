\chapter{Analysis of Functions of One Variable}

This chapter is not introduced, as the reader should be familiar with it. Regarding derivation, the following topics should be known:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Derivation of polynomials, root functions, trigonometric functions, logarithms and exponential functions,
    \item Product rule, quotient rule, chain rule,
    \item Derivation of a function elevated to another function,
    \begin{align*}
        \frac{d}{dx} \left( f(x)^{g(x)} \right) &= \dfrac{d}{dx} \left( e^{\ln\left(f(x)^{g(x)}\right)} \right) = \dfrac{d}{dx} \left( e^{g(x) \cdot \ln\left(f(x)\right)} \right) = e^{g(x) \cdot \ln\left(f(x)\right)} \cdot \dfrac{d}{dx} \left( g(x) \cdot \ln\left(f(x)\right) \right)
        =\\&= f(x)^{g(x)} \cdot \left( g'(x) \cdot \ln\left(f(x)\right) + g(x) \cdot \dfrac{f'(x)}{f(x)} \right).
    \end{align*}
    \item Curve discussion.
\end{itemize}

Regarding integration, the following topics should be known:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Integration of polynomials, root functions, trigonometric functions, logarithms and exponential functions,
    \item Integration by parts, substitution rule,
\end{itemize}
